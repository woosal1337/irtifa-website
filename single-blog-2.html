Drone Software (Computer Vision)

For the agriculture competition of Teknofest, we, as the İrtifa team, studied the methods of the computer vision in OpenCV to utilize them in our project.

  • #Software
  • #Drone
  • #IrtifaTeam

"We worked individually to accomplish different tasks, but also added a collaborative aspect whenever necessary." - Fatma Büşra Yaman

One of the main tasks to solve was the detection of the illnesses on the trees and the fruits, in particular, cherry and apple trees. However, to do this, at first detecting the trees was necessary, and this was solved using optical flow to block out the trees and HSV colour space to further define. These two methods complemented each other since optical flow firstly eliminated the similarly-coloured ground with grass by using the distance from the drone, and HSV mask selected the tree by building on top of it.

After that, the part to classify the diseases came. For this purpose, HSV colour space was used again. One of the diseases has characteristic black and brown blotches on leaves. For this particular one, an algorithm to mask the differently coloured parts and masking the remaining parts, effectively only leaving the parts with the disease was written. This was furthered by adding the ability to contour the diseased parts and calculating the total area to conclude if the leaves were diseased and, consequently, the tree. A similar illness was seen on the fruits, too, the same algorithm could be used for green apples, but for red apples and cherries, it is necessary to change the mask colours to a suitable range to detect red at first. Another disease is marked by white cocoons and webs which could be perceived by using HSV algorithm to filter out the colours other than white. Other illnesses will be added as the time goes on for the detection of a wider spectrum of diseases.

Another topic was the detection of humans and animals in the field. For this reason, deep learning algorithms with Tensorflow will be used. An appropriate algorithm will be chosen for the task at hand with the highest accuracy to speed ratio. Since the people and animals will be detected from above it will make it a little bit harder but with the right training, using data from thousands of hectares of field, it is possible.

The last part is to differentiate the foreign objects left or forgotten on the field and to do this, another HSV mask will be used, and the objects will be shown on the website for the farmers and workers to discern.
